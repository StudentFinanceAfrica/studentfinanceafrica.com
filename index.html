Why Student Finance Africa?

We are accessible.

  1. Accessing financing for education is difficult across Africa. Banks offer student loans but require collateral and an existing banking relationship with them. Often, their repayment periods are short, and their loans don’t cover the total amount of tuition you need. Also, many use traditional credit scores, and most people in Africa (80%!) don’t have a credit history. To banks, lending to students, and often their parents is risky.

  2. Government programs are helpful, but often only for the poor. Even if you qualify for these programs, it’s rarely enough to cover a year of school fees.

  3. SFA works with you and your family to meet your needs through an innovative financing approach. We break down the barriers to accessing financing. Families need a better loan solution that can make education more accessible. SFA loans are built to meet your cash flow needs, minus the stress and complications.

How Student Finance Africa Works

We partner with leading schools across Africa that are proven to help students get successfully employed. Currently, we are adding programs regularly. If you don’t see your program, let us know.

Applying for an SFA loan is easy — here is how it works.

  1. Application

    We require that a student applies with a primary borrower such as a parent or guardian. Access the SFA loan application above.

  2. Selection & Funding

    SFA provides students and parents/guardians with loans by using alternative data and a combination of demographic, geographic, financial, and other social indicators.

  3. Repayment

    You start paying back the loan while you are in school in order to build your credit profile. Payment continues after you graduate and start working.

    • SFA lends up to 80% of the needed amount for attending classes.

    • SFA pays the educational institution on behalf of its borrowers

    • Monthly payments are made directly to SFA.

    • Once monthly payments have been made and the student maintains satisfactory grades, SFA releases the tuition needed for the next term directly to the school.
